PCA Components Explained

PC1 (Momentum Strength) → RSI & MACD → Captures market momentum.

PC2 (Trend Strength) → ADX & MACD → Captures sustained trend movements.

PC3 (Divergences & Mean Reversions) → MACD & RSI negative correlation.

Understanding PC3 in Detail:

Positive PC3 = High MACD, Low RSI → Strong Momentum but Overbought

  • Market is moving up strongly, but RSI suggests it's overbought
  • Warning of an upcoming correction or pullback
  • Look for exhaustion signals (RSI above 70, price stalling)

Negative PC3 = Low MACD, High RSI → Weak Momentum but Oversold

  • Market has weak momentum, but RSI is recovering from oversold conditions
  • Market may be bottoming out or setting up for a reversal
  • Look for long opportunities when PC1 is turning positive
Trading Strategy
📊 Market Regime Clustering
  • If PC1 is high and PC2 is high → Strong trending market (breakout strategy).
  • If PC1 is high but PC2 is low → Momentum without a trend (range breakout).
  • If PC1 and PC2 are both low → Choppy/noisy market (avoid trading).
📈 Trading Signals
  • Long trades when PC1 crosses above zero & PC2 is rising.
  • Short trades when PC1 is negative & PC2 is dropping.
  • Avoid trading when PC3 is high (mean reversion environment).
Advanced PC3 Strategies
🔍 Filter Out Bad Trades
  • If PC3 is high → Avoid chasing momentum trades
  • If PC3 is low → Avoid shorting breakdowns
↔️ Divergence-Based Entries
  • Go Long when PC3 is negative (oversold RSI, weak MACD)
  • Go Short when PC3 is positive (overbought RSI, strong MACD)
🔄 Combined PCA Analysis
  • If PC1 is rising, PC2 is strong, but PC3 is positive → Momentum may be topping out (reduce position size)
  • If PC1 is weak, PC2 is flat, but PC3 is negative → Momentum may be bottoming out (look for reversals)
